Debit and Credit cards

Plastic money nothing but the credit and debit cards are made out of plastic. It is a new and easy way of paying for goods and services. It was introduced in the early 1950s and is an essential form of ready money which reduces the risk of handling a huge amount of cash. Plastic bankonotes were introduced in Australia.

Plastic money

David Solomon, an Australian scientist worked in collaboration with the Reserve Bank of Australia to design the plastic money. Debit cards entered India in 1998 and they currently account for almost 95 per cent of the total number of cards in circulation.

Unlike credit and charge cards, payments using a debit card are immediately transferred from the cardholder's designated bank account, instead of them paying the money back at a later date. Debit cards usually also allow for instant withdrawal of cash, acting as the ATM card for withdrawing cash.
